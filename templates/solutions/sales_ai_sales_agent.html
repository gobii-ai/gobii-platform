Buying guide

Which type of AI sales agent should you buy?

The label gets used for very different products. Match the agent to the job: CRM upkeep, outbound volume, calls, or source-linked research that a seller can review before anything reaches a buyer.

CRM-native agents

Best for updating records, summarizing activity, and scoring accounts inside Salesforce, HubSpot, or another CRM. Gobii can prepare the research and fields before that handoff.

Outbound BDR agents

Best for high-volume prospecting and sequencing. Gobii fits when you want review-ready lead research and message angles, not unreviewed auto-sending.

Voice and calling agents

Best for phone qualification, call routing, or meeting booking. This Gobii workflow is not an autonomous caller or WhatsApp sender; it prepares briefs and handoff data for approved tools.

Research workflow agents

Best for browsing approved sources, enriching leads, filling sheets, triggering webhooks, and producing source-linked account briefs. This is where Lead Hunter is designed to start.

Workflow ownership

Use an AI sales employee where repetition slows the team down

Gobii is not positioned as a black-box replacement for sales judgment. It is a persistent AI teammate that handles the research and preparation around outbound work so sellers can spend more time on the parts that need context and trust.

Inputs

ICP criteria, priority accounts, connected sheets, source lists, CRM exports, market notes, and past lead feedback.

Actions

Search sources, inspect pages, enrich records, compare fit, summarize findings, and prepare review-ready outputs.

Outputs

Lead lists, account briefs, source URLs, fit notes, enrichment fields, outreach drafts, and handoff summaries.

Review

Sales or RevOps checks fit, verifies sources, edits messaging, and decides which prospects should enter active outreach.

Where it fits

Automate the middle of sales research, not the human relationship

Sales teams often have tools for databases, CRM, sequencing, and calls. The slow part is the connective work between those systems: finding context, checking fit, cleaning records, and keeping handoffs current. That is where an AI sales agent can be useful.

CRM handoff

Prepare reviewed fields, fit notes, source URLs, and account context for Salesforce, HubSpot, or CRM-ready imports.

Sheets and CSV

Write structured batches to Google Sheets or CSV with reviewer status, disqualifiers, enrichment fields, and links back to sources.

Webhooks

Trigger work from form fills, CRM lists, meeting events, or scheduled checks, then send approved results downstream.

Sales engagement

Prepare talking points and drafts for Outreach, Salesloft, Apollo, or similar tools while keeping send decisions with the team.

When not to use one

  • Do not use an AI sales agent for unreviewed outreach when accuracy, consent, or brand voice needs a human check.
  • Do not delegate relationship calls, negotiation, sensitive account strategy, or compliance decisions to an unattended workflow.
  • Do not start with vague goals. The agent needs clear inputs, allowed sources, quality criteria, and output expectations.

Step 1

Define the ICP

Give the agent industries, titles, geography, company size, tech stack, buying signals, exclusions, and examples of good leads.

Step 2

Choose sources

Limit the workflow to approved websites, sheets, company lists, databases, CRM exports, or webhook-fed inputs.

Step 3

Set review rules

Decide who reviews lead quality, what fields matter, and which outputs are allowed to move downstream.

Step 4

Tune the workflow

Use seller feedback to narrow sources, update disqualifiers, change cadence, and improve the handoff format.
