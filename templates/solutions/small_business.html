Questions

Small-business AI, with the owner still in control

Gobii is best for recurring work that should be prepared, organized, and reviewed before it affects customers, vendors, money, or reputation.

What can AI agents do for a small business?

Gobii agents can help with recurring work that normally takes browser research, spreadsheets, comparison, follow-up, or record updates. Common workflows include lead research, customer follow-up, vendor monitoring, competitor tracking, directory audits, review summaries, spreadsheet cleanup, candidate research, weekly reporting, and draft communications for review.

How is Gobii different from ChatGPT?

ChatGPT is usually a place you go to ask a question. Gobii is built around persistent AI coworkers that can be given a job, contacted through email, SMS, or chat, and run work through browsers, files, schedules, and connected tools. The useful difference is follow-through: Gobii can keep working until there is a source-linked output, file, draft, or next step to review.

How is Gobii different from Zapier or n8n?

Zapier and n8n are strongest when the process is predictable: when this happens, do that. Small-business work often starts with incomplete data, changing websites, and judgment calls. Gobii is better suited for the messy steps around apps: browsing websites, comparing pages, researching context, cleaning spreadsheets, and preparing work for a person to approve.

How is Gobii different from hiring a virtual assistant?

A virtual assistant is flexible, human, and often valuable for relationship-heavy work. Gobii is better for repeatable research, monitoring, cleanup, and reporting that should happen the same way every day or week. Many teams will use both: Gobii prepares the evidence and drafts, while people handle judgment, relationships, and sensitive decisions.

Do I need to know how to code?

No. You can brief a Gobii in plain language. Tell it the job, approved sources, output format, schedule, and review rules. Technical teams can go deeper with APIs and integrations, but small businesses can start with a worker and a clear task.

Can Gobii work with my CRM, spreadsheets, website, inbox, or project tools?

Gobii is designed to work around the tools you already use. Depending on your setup, agents can use browsers, files, spreadsheets, webhooks, email, SMS, and connected business systems. The small-business pattern is simple: define what the agent may access, where results should go, and who reviews the output.

Can Gobii contact customers?

Gobii can draft customer-facing messages and help with follow-up workflows, but sensitive outreach should be reviewed by a human. For most small businesses, the safest first step is to let Gobii prepare replies, reminders, notes, and source context, then have a person approve what gets sent.

How do approvals work?

You decide where approval is required. A Gobii can gather data, update non-sensitive records, draft a message, or prepare a report on its own, then pause for review before anything important goes to a customer, vendor, applicant, financial system, or public channel.

What should I automate first?

Start with a task that is repetitive, easy to judge, and annoying enough that it keeps getting delayed. Good first workflows include weekly lead research, review monitoring, vendor price checks, spreadsheet cleanup, local directory audits, and owner briefings.

Is Gobii safe for sensitive business data?

Gobii workflows should be set up with approved sources, scoped credentials, clear review steps, and careful handling of customer or business data. Agents should only access what they need for the job, and humans should review outputs before decisions that affect money, customers, hiring, compliance, or reputation.

Can I use Gobii if I only have one or two employees?

Yes. Gobii is useful when the same person is selling, serving customers, chasing vendors, checking spreadsheets, and doing admin after hours. You can start with one recurring job and add more as you learn where it helps.
