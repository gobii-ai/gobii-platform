Lindy vs Gobii: AI assistant or AI employee?

Comparing Lindy vs Gobii? Lindy is a strong AI assistant for inbox, meetings, calendar, follow-ups, and no-code workflows. Gobii is built for teams that need persistent AI employees with real browser sessions, files, structured data, schedules, approvals, and finished operational outputs.

Executive summary

Lindy helps manage the workday. Gobii helps teams delegate recurring web work.

Lindy is closest to Gobii when buyers are evaluating AI that can do work, not just answer questions. Lindy has custom agents, workflows, triggers, actions, memory, web browsing, computer use, phone agents, and human approval flows.

Gobii starts from a different operating model. A Gobii agent is an always-on AI employee with its own identity, browser environment, files, structured state, schedules, approvals, and channels for email, SMS, chat, webhooks, and API work.

The honest Gobii vs Lindy comparison is not whether Lindy has agents. It does. The sharper question is whether your team wants an AI executive assistant for inbox and meeting work, or persistent AI employees that can operate websites, maintain sessions, collect data, and produce deliverables over time.

Which should you choose?

Pick based on the job you are delegating.

Choose Lindy if...

You want an AI assistant for email triage, drafting, scheduling, meeting prep, notes, follow-ups, reminders, iMessage or SMS delegation, voice workflows, and no-code assistant automations around your workday.

Choose Gobii if...

You want AI employees for recurring business operations that require real browser sessions, logged-in website work, files, structured data, schedules, approvals, reports, CSVs, PDFs, and cloud or self-hosted deployment control.

Key finding

Lindy is a polished AI assistant. Gobii is a browser-native AI employee platform.

Lindy should not be dismissed as a chatbot. It has custom agents, triggers, actions, memory, web browsing, computer use, phone workflows, monitoring, and approval paths. It is especially strong when the work maps to an assistant that helps one person or team manage communications and meetings.

Gobii is stronger when the work looks like recurring operations: log into a portal, gather records, compare websites, update a sheet or database, download files, create a report, send a CSV, and repeat the job on a schedule or when an event arrives.

Lindy's genuine strength: the executive assistant experience

Lindy's public product story is tightly focused on the daily work surface: inbox triage, drafting, scheduling, meeting notes, prep, reminders, follow-ups, and text-based delegation from iMessage or SMS.

For a founder, executive, or individual contributor who mostly wants one AI assistant for the workday, Lindy may feel more immediately tailored than a platform built around business operations.

Gobii's edge: browser-native operations

Lindy has Web Browser actions and Computer Use, so the fair comparison is not whether Lindy can touch the web. It can.

Gobii's advantage is that browser execution is the center of the product: real web sessions, forms, clicks, downloads, screenshots, files, structured outputs, and recurring work wrapped in a durable agent lifecycle.

A complete working environment

Custom Lindy agents are built from workflows, steps, integrations, and memory. That is a powerful model for no-code automation and assistant work.

Gobii gives each AI employee the infrastructure to do the job over time: a browser, files, structured data, schedules, approvals, channels, and outputs that are meant to be reviewed, shared, and routed through a business process.

More infrastructure control

Lindy has a credible public security posture, including encryption, enterprise controls, and compliance claims. Gobii should not frame Lindy as weak on security.

Gobii's stronger claim is control: self-hosted source code, cloud deployment, dedicated enterprise infrastructure, per-agent sandboxing, encrypted secrets, audit trails, and browser-agent-specific boundaries.

Where Lindy fits

A strong choice for assistant-led workday automation.

Lindy deserves serious consideration if your primary need is an AI assistant for inbox, meetings, calendar, reminders, SMS or iMessage access, and meeting follow-up. It is also a credible option when you want a no-code workflow builder with triggers, actions, conditions, integrations, memory, monitoring, and approvals.

It is especially compelling when the workflow is person-centered: draft this reply, reschedule this meeting, take notes, prep me for a call, follow up with action items, respond to a text, route a Slack message, or run a lightweight custom workflow from a trigger.

Where Gobii fits

A stronger fit for persistent business operations.

Gobii is purpose-built for work that a human teammate would otherwise do across websites, portals, files, data, and follow-up loops. A Gobii agent can keep context, preserve browser sessions, maintain structured records, use files, wake from schedules or events, and return finished work.

For teams searching for a Gobii vs Lindy comparison or a Lindy alternative for browser-native AI work, the practical distinction is whether you want an assistant to manage the workday, or AI employees that take on recurring operational responsibilities.

  • Recruiting and candidate sourcing
  • Vendor and supplier checks

  • Customer health summaries

Bottom line

Lindy is excellent for assistant workflows. Gobii is built for always-on AI employees.

If you mostly need help with inbox, meetings, scheduling, notes, follow-ups, SMS, iMessage, and phone-agent workflows, Lindy is worth evaluating. If you need AI employees that operate the web, maintain browser sessions, use files and structured data, run recurring business workflows, and deliver operational artifacts, Gobii is the stronger fit.
