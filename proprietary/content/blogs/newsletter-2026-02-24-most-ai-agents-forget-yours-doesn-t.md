Every AI agent has a dirty secret: they forget.

Send 100 messages. By message 50, the early ones are gone. By message 80, the agent is guessing. By message 100, it's like talking to a stranger who read your CliffsNotes.

It's called context rot — and it's the reason most AI agents feel dumber the longer you use them.

Your Gobii doesn't have this problem anymore.

We just shipped Infinite Context Recall. Your agent can now reach back to the very first message you ever sent it — and everything in between. Every instruction. Every preference. Every correction. Every conversation.

Not a summary. Not a "best effort." The actual history.

Here's why this matters more than you think:

→ Agent drift is dead. Your agent doesn't slowly "forget" how you like things done. The tone, format, and preferences you set on day one still hold on day 100.

→ Compounding knowledge. The more you work with your agent, the smarter it gets — because it remembers everything you've taught it. That offhand correction three weeks ago? Still in there.

→ No more re-explaining. "I already told you this" is something you'll never have to say again. Your agent knows what you told it. All of it.

→ Trust builds over time. When your agent remembers your context perfectly, you stop second-guessing it and start relying on it. That's the unlock.

Most AI tools get worse the more you use them. Context windows fill up, old messages get dropped, and you're back to square one.

Gobii agents get better the more you use them. Because they never forget.
